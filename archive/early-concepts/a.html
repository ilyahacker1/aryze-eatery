Our story · Phoenix, 2026

We built the
drive-thru we
actually wanted
to roll through.

A drive-thru window is the most honest restaurant format there is. You're in. You're out. There's no atmosphere to hide behind. The food has to be the whole point.

For thirty years the only healthy option on the way to work in Phoenix was the bottle of water you bought next to the gas pump. Drive-thru meant something between salty, fried, and fluorescent. We refused to accept that as a category.

So in spring 2026, we opened a small white-brick building at the corner of South Central and Mojave. We put a sun on the wall, a flag on the roof, and a menu inside that we'd be willing to feed our own kids: organic acaí, line-caught ahi, single-origin coffee, cold-pressed juice, bone broth, breakfast burritos with real eggs.

That building is now Aryze #001. We're hiring for #002. And we're writing the playbook for what a healthy national drive-thru actually feels like — one window, one neighborhood, one bowl at a time.
